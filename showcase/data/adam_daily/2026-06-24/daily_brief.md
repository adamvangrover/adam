# Adam OS Daily Market Brief: Market Mayhem

**Date:** 2026-06-24 (Reporting on June 23 Close)

## Market Overview

A pervasive tech-led sell-off dictated Tuesday's session, forcing global equities lower as investors recalibrated their exposure to AI-related momentum and digested a hawkish Federal Reserve narrative. The Nasdaq Composite bore the brunt of the retraction, tumbling 2.2% to 25,587.04, while the S&P 500 shed 1.4% to close at 7,365.46. Credit markets remain relatively insulated, with broadly syndicated loan yields holding steady despite the elevated equity volatility.

## Global News Scan

* **United States:** Big Tech faced significant downward pressure amid rising concerns over prolonged high interest rates and the sustainability of massive capital expenditures in AI infrastructure.
* **Europe:** The FTSE 100 slipped to 10,429 amid political uncertainty in the U.K. and a broader global risk-off sentiment.
* **Asia-Pacific:** Japan's Nikkei 225 plummeted 3.6% to 69,788.38, closing below 70,000 for the first time in a week as profit-taking swept through semiconductor equities. South Korea's KOSPI faced even steeper declines, triggering a temporary trading halt.
* **Commodities & Rates:** The U.S. 10-Year Treasury yield edged down to 4.49%. Gold broke below the $4,100 threshold, pressured by a strengthening U.S. dollar and an 88% market-implied probability of a Fed rate hike by year-end.
