# Daily Brief: 2026-07-16

## Market Overview
The first quarter of 2026 represents a sharp structural shift in institutional asset management. The definitive transition of power at Berkshire Hathaway into the "Abel Era" has brought a highly concentrated, deliberate approach to capital reallocation, clearing out mid-conviction legacy positions in favor of high-conviction structural bets. The consensus on Artificial Intelligence has fractured into a distinct multi-layer playbook. Institutional capital is aggressively separating the Hardware and Foundry Layer from the SaaS/Application Layer.

## Macro Indicators
*   **Hardware Inflows:** Capital is hyper-concentrating in the physical infrastructure and foundry layer—specifically memory, foundry, and datacenters (Micron, Oracle, Broadcom, TSMC).
*   **SaaS Liquidations:** Downstream software-as-a-service (SaaS) plays are systematically abandoned.
*   **Real Assets:** Macro funds are structural buyers of hard commodities to offset the long-duration risk of their tech books.

## Risk Radar
*   **Volatility Shock in the AI Stack:** The smart money has officially abandoned downstream software-as-a-service (SaaS) plays.
*   **Portfolio Pruning:** Liquidating mid-conviction, high-multiple names. If a company does not possess an ironclad, unassailable moat, it is being converted to cash or concentrated into top-tier sovereign alpha.
