The Degradation of Credit Structures

TLBs, heavily favored by yield-seeking institutional investors to fund private equity leveraged buyouts (LBOs), feature minimal amortization and "covenant-lite" architectures. These structures rely entirely on incurrence covenants, which only test financial health if a borrower attempts a restricted action, thereby removing the proactive monitoring required to prevent catastrophic defaults.
