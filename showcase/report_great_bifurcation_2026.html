1. The Anatomy of the "SaaSpocalypse"

The immediate catalyst for the Q1 2026 sell-off—a precipitous drop in legal technology and BPO stocks following the release of Anthropic’s "Claude Cowork" and Alphabet’s "Project Genie"—has crystallized fears regarding the deflationary impact of AI on software revenue.

The market is attempting to price the risk of "seat compression," the theory that agentic AI will reduce human headcount, thereby collapsing the TAM for vendors reliant on per-user pricing.

Key Strategic Convictions for 2026:

  • The Divergence is Real: "AI-Native" platforms (CrowdStrike, Palantir) are seeing valuation premiums expand, while "Legacy Seat" vendors (Legal, BPO) face valuation compression of 15-20%.
  • Debt Markets Signaling Liquidity: A disconnect exists between liquid software loans (down ~3%) and private credit portfolios (stable). This reflects liquidity fears rather than systemic deterioration.
  • "Trough of Disillusionment" is a Buy Signal: Speculative spending is replaced by rigorous ROI demands. This favors incumbents (Microsoft, ServiceNow) who deliver AI as a seamless feature upgrade.
  • Operational Resilience: Empirical data from 2025 indicates AI tools currently increase software maintenance complexity, benefiting orchestration platforms like GitLab.

3. Dissecting the Disruption

Seat Compression vs. The J-Curve: The bear case rests on "seat compression" (fewer humans = fewer licenses). This is valid for transactional roles. However, for complex knowledge work, the METR Study (2025) found that using early-2025 AI tools made developers 19% slower on complex tasks due to the increased cognitive load of supervision. This is bullish for platforms managing code lifecycle (GitLab, GitHub).

The "AI Tariff": Gartner projects 2026 AI spending to surge 44% to $2.52T, crowding out general software spending (+14.7%). CIOs are funding AI by cutting "shelfware".
