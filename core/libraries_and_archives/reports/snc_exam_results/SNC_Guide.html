Shared National Credit (SNC) Exam Preparation Guide

A Comprehensive Handbook for Bank Analysts

1. Introduction to SNC Exams

Purpose

The Shared National Credit (SNC) Program reviews large syndicated loans ($100M+ shared by 3+ institutions) to provide consistent classification across regulatory agencies (OCC, Fed, FDIC). The exam aims to:

  • Assess credit quality and risk management for large exposures.
  • Identify trends in syndicated lending.
  • Ensure banks have adequate capital and reserves.
  • Promote sound underwriting and credit administration.

Scope

  • Loan Size: Credits aggregating $100 million or more.
  • Participants: Shared by three or more federally supervised institutions.
  • Focus: The assigned rating applies to all banks in the syndicate.

Importance for Your Institution

  • Regulatory Scrutiny: Directly impacts CAMELS ratings and capital adequacy.
  • Provisioning & Capital: Criticized assets require higher reserves, impacting profitability.
  • Reputational Risk: Poor results can damage market and regulatory reputation.
  • Risk Management Improvement: Provides feedback for strengthening internal processes.

2. Pre-Exam Preparation: Documentation is Key

Thorough, accurate, and well-organized documentation is the cornerstone of a successful SNC exam.

2.1. Essential Credit File Components

Ensure each file is comprehensive. Key components include:

  • Credit Approval Documents: Original approvals, amendments, waivers, and timely annual reviews.
  • Borrower Information: Legal name, structure, organizational chart, and ownership details.
  • Loan Documentation: Signed agreements, security/guarantee docs, notes, and term sheets.
  • Financial Statements & Analysis: 3-5 years of audited historicals, interims, financial spreads, well-supported projections, and ratio analysis.
  • Collateral Information (If Secured): Descriptions, current valuations, lien perfection docs, and updated LTV calculations.
  • Industry & Market Analysis: Borrower's industry, market position, and key risks.
  • Ongoing Monitoring: Call reports, internal risk rating history, covenant tracking, and watchlist reports.
  • Communication Records: Key correspondence with borrower, agent, and syndicate members.

2.2. Financial Analysis & Spreading Standards

  • Accuracy: Reconcile spreads to source documents.
  • Consistency: Apply consistent accounting treatments and adjustments.
  • Adjustments: Clearly document and justify all non-GAAP adjustments (e.g., EBITDA add-backs).
  • Projections: Base on well-supported assumptions and include stress tests.

2.3. Internal Rating Process & Justification

  • Clear Rationale: The basis for the risk rating must be clearly articulated and linked to bank methodology.
  • Timeliness: Ratings should be changed promptly when conditions change.
  • Objectivity: Ratings must be grounded in an objective assessment of credit risk.

Pro-Tip: Create a “Pre-Exam Checklist” for your key SNC files. Review them against this checklist well before the exam to identify and remediate any gaps proactively.

3. Understanding and Applying SNC Guidelines

A deep understanding of the SNC definitions is crucial. Your internal ratings should align with these principles.

3.1. Key SNC Definitions (Summarized)

  • Pass: Sound quality, minimal risk. Repayment expected as agreed.
  • Special Mention (SM): Potential weaknesses that deserve close attention.
  • Substandard: Inadequately protected by current worth/paying capacity. Well-defined weakness that jeopardizes orderly liquidation.
  • Doubtful: Collection or liquidation in full is highly questionable and improbable.
  • Loss: Considered uncollectible.

3.2. Core Focus Areas for Examiners

  • Repayment Capacity: Historical and projected cash flow sustainability.
  • Collateral: Current valuation, lien perfection, and liquidity.
  • Capital Structure & Leverage: Debt-to-Equity, tangible net worth, and debt composition.
  • Liquidity: Working capital and access to facilities.
  • Management & Sponsorship: Experience, track record, and sponsor support.
  • Covenant Compliance: Tracking of breaches, waivers, and amendments.

4. Internal Reporting and SNC Data Submission

4.1. Accuracy of Data Tapes

Errors or inconsistencies in submitted data tapes create a poor first impression. Verify key data points and reconcile totals to internal systems.

4.2. Internal Pre-Review / Mock Exams

Conducting an internal mock exam is invaluable. It helps identify potential criticisms, strengthen files, and prepare analysts to defend their credits.

5. The SNC Exam Process

5.1. Initial Information Requests (IIRs)

Respond accurately, completely, and on time to requests for files, data, and policies.

5.2. Examiner Interactions

Be prepared to discuss each credit. Maintain a professional, cooperative, and transparent demeanor.

5.3. Preliminary Findings

This is a critical opportunity to provide additional information or present counterarguments before final decisions are made.

6. Defending Your Ratings: Building a Cohesive Narrative

Your ability to articulate a clear, fact-based narrative is paramount when a rating is questioned.

6.1. Structuring Your Defense

  • Acknowledge and Validate: Acknowledge valid points raised by the examiner.
  • State Your Thesis: Clearly state your rating and overall assessment.
  • Present Key Facts: Walk through risk areas with specific, current data.
  • Address Weaknesses Proactively: Discuss known weaknesses and their mitigants.
  • Justify Reliance: Be prepared for deep scrutiny on collateral or sponsor support.

6.2. Pitching a Cohesive Narrative

  • Know Your Story: Have a concise summary for each credit.
  • Consistency: Ensure your verbal narrative matches the written documentation.
  • Connect the Dots: Explain how different pieces of information fit together.

7. Responding to Examiner Queries Effectively

  • Be Clear, Concise, Confident: Listen carefully, be direct, and ground answers in facts. If you don't know, offer to find out.
  • Provide Documentation Promptly: Be able to locate supporting documents quickly in your well-organized files.
  • Follow Protocol: Understand and follow your institution's policy for handling disagreements with examiners.

8. Post-Exam: Addressing Findings and Continuous Improvement

  • Review Exam Report: Understand all findings and required actions.
  • Remediate Deficiencies: Develop and execute action plans to address weaknesses.
  • Lessons Learned: Conduct an internal post-mortem to improve for the future.
  • Update Policies: If needed, update policies and procedures and train staff accordingly.

9. Tips & Tricks from the Trenches

No Surprises: Know your portfolio's weaknesses and downgrade proactively if warranted.

Agent Bank Communication: Maintain good communication with the agent bank if you are a participant.

Understand the Macro Environment: Be aware of economic trends impacting your borrowers.

Don't Hide Bad News: Address problems head-on in documentation and discussions.

Prepare Talking Points: For key or contentious credits, prepare concise talking points.

Stay Calm and Professional: Maintain composure throughout the exam process.

This guide provides a framework for preparing for and navigating SNC exams. Effective preparation, robust documentation, and clear communication are essential for a successful outcome.
