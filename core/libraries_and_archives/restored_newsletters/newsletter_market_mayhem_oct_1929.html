Vol. I, No. 1 — "The End of an Era"

MARKET MAYHEM

Date: October 29, 1929
Location: Wall Street, NYC
Price: Two Cents
LATEST TICKER: RCA 26... GE 210... US STEEL 174... (TAPE 2 HOURS LATE)

PANIC!

16 Million Shares Dumped on Market — Billions in Paper Wealth Evaporates

NEW YORK — The "New Era" of permanent prosperity has come to a shattering, calamitous end. Today, in a frenzy of selling that overwhelmed the ticker tape and broke the spirit of the speculator class, the stock market collapsed.

From the opening bell, a terrified deluge of sell orders flooded the floor of the Exchange. Prices did not merely decline; they melted away. RCA, the darling of the age, plummeted 26 points. General Electric shed 28. Even the mighty U.S. Steel was not spared.

"It is a slaughter of the innocents," remarked one weeping trader outside 11 Wall Street. The margin clerks have been busy all afternoon, sending out the dreaded calls that no one can answer.

"The ticker is running four hours late. No one knows the price of anything. We are flying blind into the abyss."

Rumors swirl of suicides, though few are confirmed. Crowds have gathered at Trinity Church, praying for salvation not of their souls, but of their portfolios. The bankers—Morgan, Mitchell, Potter—met at noon, but this time, there was no bid to stop the bleeding. The pool has dried up.

President Hoover assures us that "the fundamental business of the country is on a sound and prosperous basis." But looking at the sea of red ink and broken dreams on the floor today, one wonders if the fundamental business of the country was merely gambling all along.
